# Announcing FluidCalendar Version 2: Starting With Booking Links

I'm making a significant shift in my development focus: all my efforts are now going into building Version 2 of FluidCalendar.

For context, FluidCalendar is an open-source alternative to Motion — a calendar and task management application designed to help professionals actually manage their time, not just view it.

Today marks the first major Version 2 release: a complete booking system.

---

## What We're Solving

The modern professional's scheduling workflow is fragmented:

- Calendar apps show your schedule
- Task managers track your work
- Scheduling tools handle bookings
- None of them integrate properly

When someone wants to book time with you, your "availability" in a scheduling tool doesn't account for the tasks you need to complete, the focus time you've blocked, or events in secondary calendars.

---

## The New Booking System

FluidCalendar's booking links integrate directly with your calendar ecosystem:

**Core Features:**
- Public booking pages with customizable URLs
- Real-time availability checking across Google, Outlook, and CalDAV calendars
- Automatic timezone detection for guests
- Configurable meeting durations, buffer times, and booking windows
- Email confirmations with calendar attachments
- Self-service cancellation for guests

**What Makes It Different:**
The availability engine doesn't just check one calendar — it aggregates availability across all your selected calendars, respects your defined working hours, and accounts for existing bookings. The result is availability that reflects your actual schedule.

---

## On Open Source and SaaS

I'm currently prioritizing the SaaS version. The reasoning:

1. Direct user feedback enables faster iteration
2. Production monitoring catches issues early
3. Stable features can then be backported to the open-source version

The commitment to open source remains. Self-hosted users will receive the same functionality — the SaaS version simply serves as the testing ground.
